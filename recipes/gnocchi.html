Gnocchi & Sauce Class (04/21/25)

← Back to recipe list

Recipe Overview

  • Servings: 5
  • Prep Time: 60 minutes
  • Cook Time: 40 minutes
  • Total Time: 1 hour 40 minutes

    • Homemade Sauce Ingredients

    • 1 Can (794 g / 1 lb) whole peeled tomatoes
    • 1 Medium onion, diced
    • 1 Carrot, diced
    • 1 Celery stalk, diced
    • 1 tsp salt
    • 1 tbsp water
    • 1 tbsp olive oil
    • Optional: Fresh basil

    Gnocchi Ingredients

    • 1 kg (about 2.2 lb) russet potatoes
    • 500 g (about 4 cups) all-purpose flour
    • 1 tsp salt
    • Optional: 1 egg. Ignore to make the gnocchi lighter.
    • Optional: Grated parmigiano romano cheese for serving

    Sauce Instructions

    1. Prep: Dice the onion, carrot, and celery.
    2. Cook: In a saucepan, add the diced onion and water. Sauté on medium heat until the onion is translucent.
    3. Add veggies: Add the diced carrot and celery. Cook for 5-10 minutes until softened.
    4. Add tomatoes: Add the can of whole peeled tomatoes (with juice). Use a fork to break up the tomatoes.
    5. Aromatics: Add a pinch of salt, more if the tomatoes are acidic, and the basil. Cook for 5 minutes.
    6. Simmer: Simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens. Stir occasionally.
    7. Finish: Taste and adjust seasoning. Optionally add a drizzle of olive oil before serving.

    Gnocchi Instructions

    1. Boil water with salt: In a pot place enough water to cover the potatoes. Add a generous pinch of salt. Allow the water to come to a boil.
    2. Wash the potatoes:Wash off impurities but do not peel off the skin.
    3. Cook the potatoes: Boil whole potatoes with skin on until tender enough to pierce with a knife (30–40 min). Drain and cool slightly.
    4. Rice & cool: Peel while warm and pass through a potato ricer (or mash).
    5. Make the dough: Mix the potatoes with the flour and gently combine until a dough forms.
    6. Optional: Add the egg and a pinch of salt. Mix well into the dough.
    7. Shape: Divide into 4 pieces. Roll each piece into a snake like tube.
    8. Cut Cut tubes into 2 cm (~3/4 in) thick pieces.
    9. Texture Optionally roll on a fork for ridges.
    10. Rest: Rest gnocchi on a floured surface, dust some flour over them as well, for 10-20 minutes. This will help make the gnocchi stay firm while cooking.
    11. Boil water with salt: In a pot place enough water to fill half the pot. Add a generous pinch of salt. Allow the water to come to a boil.
    12. Cook: Boil in salted water; add just enough so that there's a single layer of gnocchi at the bottom of the pot. As the gnocchi start to float (~2-3 min) transfer them to a pan or plate.
    13. Serve: Finish with the homemade sauce and parmigiano!

    Tips for perfect gnocchi

    • Work while potatoes are warm so flour incorporates evenly.
    • Use as little flour as possible for tender gnocchi.
